Discovery

I observed a lot of concern with the power, speed, and frequency setting on the Epilog Laser. My suspicion was that there would be very little impact on the cut quality given how forgiving nature of cardboard. In class, Josh recommend a setting of 100% power. With the given setting of 100% power, I decided to prove how quickly the cardboard could be cut. There were multiple comments to the Slack conversation documenting speeds around 25%. I made a simple rectangle vector and began making cuts at various speeds. The key finding was that the power could be increased to a value of 46% while still generating acceptable cuts. There is one caveat I observed that could allow for higher speeds. If your paths consisted of several short segments the speeds could be increased, but no performance increase on cycletime would be observed. I am confident this is accurate because the image of the rectangle above shows the corner cutting correctly and midway through the path on the back side of the cardboard the cut fades away. This fading indicates the laser head is still accelerating and has passed a speed slow enough to cut through the cardboard.
